The 2017 TMS Student Council

Treasurer

He is the student videographer for most events, but his true passions lie in conveying a clear message to his audience and ensuring fiscal responsibility in his organizations.

Amanda Carnovale

Grade 11 Class Representative

Amanda is the Grade 11 Class Representative on Student Council for this school year and one of two intramurals commissioners for the school. She has won national design awards and is passionate about creativity in leadership. Effective communication is her strongest value in her position at TMS, requiring her to conduct peer-to-peer research, as well as event promotion within her grade.

Andrea Chang

Citizenship Chair

She’s also the student leader of the Social Justice club, which has many students in grades 7 and 8, as well as senior grades. Our Amazing Teachers & Alumni

The non-student side of things.

Ashley Bailey

Student Life Coordinator

Ashley Bailey is the Student Life Coordinator at TMS, and star of the Youtube Channel MsBaileySays. Over the past three years, she has developed a leadership continuum at TMS to encourage leaders to thrive and achieve transformational goals. She believes that hard skills, like the Stanford Design Cycle and Budgeting compliment soft skills like Social Intelligence and classroom management towards effective leadership. Her vision for the conference is to collaborate with other school and student leaders to help one another through unique school challenges, as well as to celebrate successes.

Steven Alves

House Coordinator & IB Math and Physics Teacher

Steven is the Houses Coordinator at TMS, and has also had a hand in every aspect of leadership programming in the school - Outdoor Ed, Duke of Edinburgh Awards, and Student Council, for example. As a full time IB Mathematics and Physics teacher, he has learned to balance his co-curricular programming role amidst grading, teaching, and other curricular responsibilities. He oversaw the restructure of TMS Houses and revisioning of the House System over the past two years, and co-created the TMS leadership retreat to actively scaffold and support leadership skills among the students. He looks forward to engaging with other teacher-advisors and to discuss the challenges and successes around revisioning and restructuring programs to invite optimal engagement.

Adam Grotsky

President of the Society of Graduate and Professional Students - Queen's University

Adam proudly graduated from TMS as valedictorian in 2011. From there, he began a long and rewarding journey at Queen’s University. During his undergraduate degree in political studies, Adam’s passion for public service and community involvement was sparked. This led to his role as President of the Arts and Science Undergraduate Society, where he represented over 10,000 students and managed a $1 million budget. Currently, Adam is in his second year of law school and serves as President of the Society of Graduate and Professional Students at Queen’s.
