What's going on at this event?

 Each school community faces unique challenges, and celebrates unique situational successes in student leadership. The purpose of this conference is to collaborate with other schools and student leaders to help one another through unique school challenges, and to help great student leaders to network with one another and build skills together to enrich their own communities.

 Hard skills, like the Stanford Design Cycle and budgeting compliment soft skills like Social Intelligence and classroom management towards effective leadership. Our aim is that participants in this conference, while hosted by TMS, will take ownership in their groups of various challenges facing their own leadership, and collaborate with others in a similar role (e.g. Treasurer, House Captain) to solve these challenges. We hope that the “hard skill” take-aways will be transferable and concrete, and the networks will be beneficial into the future.

Leading with Finances

We will discuss effective budgeting, financial record keeping, cash flow, and sales management. Being “in the black” is an easy concept, but for financial representatives it can be a stressful tug-of-war with other leaders and decision makers. We will look at strategies for leading through financial responsibility and collaborate on solutions for individual schools, communities, and leaders.

  • Time: TBA
  • Room: 237

Young Leaders

Unique challenges face youngers students in leadership roles. Simple questions like “how to get my peers to listen to me”, as well as larger strategies like “mobilizing faculty to support student initiatives” will be discussed in this seminar. We will work on specific challenges facing the young leaders in attendance, and work towards concrete take-aways for each student.

  • Time: TBA
  • Room: 238

Social Engagement

Social engagement, or the weight of events within the social economy of organizations, is an essential factor in any program. In this seminar, we will be focusing on inclusivity and culture, student involvement, and House systems within schools. We look forward to sharing our experiences overhauling the House System for optimal social engagement at our own school, and hearing from you and your own school’s experience.

  • Time: TBA
  • Room: 203

Public Relations

In this seminar, we will explore what public relations means in the context of High School, and discuss strategies for reaching our target audience. We will also have the opportunity to address unique PR challenges in our communities. Your insights and experiences will be at the forefront of this seminar, but you will take home some transferable PR strategies to use in any group.

  • Time: TBA
  • Room: 232

Group Management

As leaders, we have to manage and organize large groups on a regular basis. We will be discussing methods of conducting meetings and managing the flow of ideas in groups. Our school has a history of different group organizational structures for meetings and executing events, including Harkness discussion circles, hierarchical organization, and vote-based decision making. We’ll discuss the pros and cons of these strategies, as well as effective implementation of them. This collaborative seminar will allow time for problem solving and strategy sharing within the group, as well as skill development.

  • Time: TBA
  • Room: 116

Creative Problem Solving

Complex problems require robust thinking to solve. In this seminar, we will be discussing design thinking processes and prototyping in a Student Leadership setting. This seminar will teach you transferable skills and processes to follow when looking for a solution that satisfies multiple stakeholders. This is a “hands on” session that discusses and develops practical skills for solid solutions.

  • Time: TBA
  • Room: Art Room (2nd floor)

Sales & Promotions

Sales, fundraising, and event promotion are important parts of student leadership and event organization. In this seminar, we hope to explore methods of presenting our endeavours that appeal to our audience and maximize the success of our activities. There will be an opportunity to discuss unique challenges for each school, and to collaborate in solving issues to improve sales and promotion in each community.

  • Time: TBA
  • Room: 114

Epic Programming

Think Bigger and Better. What makes a program or event “Epic” - what makes it impactful, worthwhile, and fun? In this seminar we will establish criteria, strategies, and the value of big programs and events, and look for solutions to school-specific problems in event conception and execution.

  • Time: TBA
  • Room: Library

Teachers Seminars

Teachers are invited to brainstorm and share their leadership development philosophies and approaches with their peers who oversee student leadership. Our first seminar will discuss the Stanford Design Cycle and other methods for solving whole-school issues in student programming and leadership. Our second seminar will discuss leadership training and development strategies within schools, and the successes and challenges we have come across with each unique community.

  • Time: TBA
  • Room: Commons
